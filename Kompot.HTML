About

Kampot is a charming riverside province in southern Cambodia known for its laid-back atmosphere, French colonial architecture, and world-famous Kampot pepper. Nestled between the Elephant Mountains and the Gulf of Thailand, it is one of Cambodia's most beloved destinations for both locals and international visitors seeking nature, culture, and relaxation.

What it is known for
  • 🌶️ World-Famous Kampot Pepper
  • 🏔️ Bokor Hill Station
  • 🏝️ Rabbit Island (Koh Tonsay)
  • 🏛️ French Colonial Architecture
📍 Top Places to Visit
🏔️
Bokor Hill Station
A mysterious French-era hilltop resort with an abandoned casino, church ruins, and spectacular coastal views.
🏝️
Rabbit Island (Koh Tonsay)
A peaceful offshore island with white sand beaches, clear water, and simple bungalows for an overnight stay.
🌶️
Pepper Plantations
Tour the famous Kampot pepper farms and learn why this pepper is considered the best in the world.
🌊
Kampot River
Take a relaxing sunset boat cruise along the river surrounded by mangroves and mountain scenery.
🏛️
Old French Quarter
Stroll through beautifully preserved French colonial streets lined with colourful buildings and cafes.
🦑
Crab Market (Kep Nearby)
Visit the famous nearby crab market for fresh seafood — a must-do for any food lover in the region.
💧
Tek Chhou Waterfall
A popular local waterfall and swimming spot surrounded by jungle — perfect for a cool dip.
🐊
Phnom Chhngok Cave Temple
An ancient cave temple with a small Hindu shrine and beautiful stalactites deep inside the mountain.
🌅
Sunset at the Riverfront
Watch the stunning sunset from Kampot's scenic riverfront — one of the most beautiful in Cambodia.
🚴
Countryside Cycling
Explore pepper farms, salt fields, and quiet villages on two wheels through the scenic Kampot countryside.
