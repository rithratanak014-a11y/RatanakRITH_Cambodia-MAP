Kandal

Khmer name: កណ្ដាល
Capital: Ta Khmau
Population: 1,264,853

About

Kandal is a province that completely surrounds the capital Phnom Penh, making it one of the most strategically important provinces in Cambodia. Its name means "Central" in Khmer. The province is known for its silk weaving traditions, the confluence of the Mekong and Tonle Sap rivers, and numerous Buddhist pagodas and historical sites.

What it is known for
  • 🧵 Silk Weaving Traditions
  • 🌊 Tonle Sap & Mekong Confluence
  • ⛩️ Ancient Pagodas
  • 🏘️ Proximity to Phnom Penh
📍 Top Places to Visit
🌊
Chatomuk (Four Faces River)
The famous confluence where the Mekong, Tonle Sap, and Bassac rivers meet — a sacred and scenic landmark.
🧵
Koh Dach Silk Island
A peaceful river island famous for traditional silk weaving — watch artisans create beautiful Khmer textiles.
⛩️
Wat Prey Veng
A historic and beautifully decorated Buddhist pagoda surrounded by lush gardens in the countryside.
🏯
Tonle Bati Temple
A popular 12th-century Angkor-era temple complex featuring Ta Prohm and Yeay Peau shrines near a scenic lake.
🏔️
Phnom Tamao Wildlife Rescue Centre
A large wildlife sanctuary and zoo rescuing endangered Cambodian animals including tigers, bears, and elephants.
🛶
River Island Boat Trips
Explore the many small islands and waterways along the Mekong and Tonle Sap by traditional wooden boat.
🌾
Rice & Vegetable Farms
Kandal's fertile floodplains produce rice and vegetables that supply much of Phnom Penh's food markets.
🎨
Traditional Craft Villages
Visit villages specializing in lacquerware, silver crafts, and hand-woven textiles kept alive for generations.
🌅
Mekong Riverside Sunsets
Watch breathtaking sunsets over the Mekong River from quiet riverbank spots away from the busy capital.
🍜
Ta Khmau Food Street
The provincial capital's food scene offers delicious local Khmer dishes, fresh river fish, and street snacks.
