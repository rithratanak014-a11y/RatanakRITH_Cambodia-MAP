About

Pailin is one of Cambodia's smallest provinces, nestled at the northern edge of the Cardamom Mountains on the western border with Thailand. Once a Khmer Rouge stronghold, it is now known for its gem mining history, tropical fruit orchards, and mountain scenery.It gained autonomous province status in 2001 after being carved out of Battambang.

What it is known for
  • 💎 Precious Gem Mining
  • 🏔 Cardamom Mountain Foothills
  • 🍋 Tropical Fruit Orchards
  • 🏛 Khmer Rouge Historical Sites
📍 Top Places to Visit
⛩
Wat Phnom Yat
A hilltop temple offering panoramic views of the valley — a sacred site and beloved landmark of Pailin City.
💎
Gem Mining Areas
Pailin is historically famous for rubies, sapphires, and gemstones extracted from its rich highland soil.
🏔
Cardamom Mountain Foothills
Scenic hills and forests reaching up to 1,164 m with cool breezes and stunning highland views.
🌊
O'Tavao Waterfall
A peaceful natural waterfall surrounded by lush forest, popular for day trips and picnics from Pailin City.
🍋
Tropical Fruit Orchards
Local farms produce abundant mangoes, durians, and rambutans — visitors can sample fresh seasonal fruit.
🌏
Thai Border — Promh Checkpoint
The province's international border gate with Thailand, drawing cross-border traders and visitors.
