About

Kep is Cambodia's smallest province and one of its most charming — a sleepy seaside town on the Gulf of Thailand that was once a beloved French colonial retreat.Famous for its iconic Crab Market and fresh blue crabs served with Kampot pepper sauce, Kep combines culinary delight with white-sand beaches, a lush national park, and the romantic ruins of abandoned colonial villas.

What it is known for
  • 🦀 Kep Crab Market (Phsar Kdam)
  • 🌶 Kampot Pepper Crab
  • 🌴 Kep National Park
  • 🏚 Abandoned French Colonial Villas
📍 Top Places to Visit
🦀
Kep Crab Market
Kep's most iconic attraction — buy fresh blue crabs and have them cooked on the spot with Kampot pepper sauce.
🌴
Kep National Park
An 8 km forest trail winding through lush jungle above the town, with viewpoints over the Gulf of Thailand.
🏖
Kep Beach
A relaxed seaside strip popular with locals and visitors, featuring the giant blue crab welcome statue.
🏚
Abandoned French Villas
Hauntingly beautiful ruins of colonial-era mansions left empty since the Khmer Rouge era.
🏝
Koh Tonsay (Rabbit Island)
A serene offshore island with quiet beaches and simple bungalows — a short boat ride from Kep.
🌶
Kampot Pepper Farms
Kep sits in the Kampot pepper growing region — visit nearby farms to see how Cambodia's famous pepper is grown.
