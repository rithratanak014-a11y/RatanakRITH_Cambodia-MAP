Population: 260,000

About

Oddar Meanchey, meaning "Victorious North," is a remote province in Cambodia's far northwest bordering Thailand.Carved from Siem Reap province in 1999, it holds deep historical significance as the last stronghold of the Khmer Rouge, where leaders including Pol Pot spent their final years. Today it is emerging as a heritage and eco-tourism destination with forests, mountains, and wildlife sanctuaries.

What it is known for
  • 🏛 Anlong Veng Khmer Rouge Sites
  • ⛰ Dangrek Mountain Range
  • 🌿 Kulen Promtep Wildlife Sanctuary
  • 🌏 Thai Border Crossings
📍 Top Places to Visit
🏛
Anlong Veng Historical Site
The last stronghold of the Khmer Rouge, including Ta Mok's house and the burial site of Pol Pot.
⛰
Dangrek Mountains
A dramatic escarpment forming Cambodia's natural border with Thailand, cloaked in dense jungle.
🌿
Kulen Promtep Wildlife Sanctuary
A biodiverse wildlife sanctuary in the southeast offering eco-tourism and forest trekking.
🌏
O'Smach Border Town
A lively border hub 41 km north of Samraong with casinos, restaurants, and hotels.
💧
La Ang Tathong Waterfall
A scenic waterfall 35 km from the provincial capital along Road 68, a refreshing natural escape.
🗿
Prasat Ta Muen Thom
An ancient Khmer temple on the Thai-Cambodian border, once a waypoint on the ancient royal road from Angkor.
