About

Preah Vihear is a large, sparsely populated province in northern Cambodia bordering Thailand. It is home to the UNESCO World Heritage Site of Prasat Preah Vihear, a magnificent 11th-century Khmer temple perched dramatically on a cliff of the Dangrek Mountains. The province also contains several other important Angkorian temples and vast protected forests.

What it is known for
  • 🏯 Prasat Preah Vihear (UNESCO)
  • ⛰ Dangrek Mountains
  • 🏛 Koh Ker Temple Complex
  • 🌿 Kulen Promtep Wildlife Sanctuary
📍 Top Places to Visit
🏯
Prasat Preah Vihear
A UNESCO World Heritage cliff-top temple with stunning views over the Cambodian plains — one of the country's most dramatic sites.
🏛
Koh Ker Temple Complex
An ancient 10th-century Angkorian capital with a towering seven-tiered pyramid temple at its centre.
⛰
Dangrek Escarpment
A dramatic cliff-edge mountain range forming the natural border with Thailand, offering sweeping panoramas.
🌿
Kulen Promtep Wildlife Sanctuary
One of Cambodia's most important dry forest habitats, home to rare birds and endangered species.
🏛
Prasat Thom
The central pyramid tower of Koh Ker — a striking 36-metre structure that once rivalled Angkor Wat.
🌾
Tbeng Meanchey Town
The quiet provincial capital serving as a base for exploring Preah Vihear's many heritage sites.
🦅
Bird Watching
The dry forests and grasslands are critical habitat for endangered vultures and giant ibis.
🏺
Scattered Khmer Ruins
Remote and rarely visited Angkorian temples hidden throughout the province's countryside.
