About

Phnom Penh is the capital and largest city of Cambodia. Situated at the confluence of the Mekong and Tonle Sap rivers, it is the country's political, economic, and cultural center. The city blends French colonial architecture with modern development and rich Khmer heritage.

What it is known for
  • 🏯 Royal Palace
  • ⛩️ Wat Phnom
  • 🏛️ National Museum
  • 🕊️ Tuol Sleng Genocide Museum
📍 Top Places to Visit
🏯
Royal Palace
The stunning royal residence featuring Khmer architecture, the Silver Pagoda, and the Emerald Buddha.
⛩️
Wat Phnom
A hilltop Buddhist temple and the most important religious site in the city, dating back to 1373.
🏛️
National Museum of Cambodia
Home to the world's largest collection of Khmer art and sculptures spanning over 1,000 years.
🕊️
Tuol Sleng Genocide Museum
A sobering memorial to victims of the Khmer Rouge, housed in the former S-21 prison.
🌅
Riverside Promenade
A scenic waterfront walkway along the Tonle Sap River — perfect for sunset strolls.
🛍️
Central Market (Psar Thmei)
An iconic Art Deco market selling everything from jewellery to street food and souvenirs.
💀
Choeung Ek Killing Fields
A deeply moving memorial and museum commemorating the victims of the Khmer Rouge regime.
🍜
Street 240 Food Scene
A lively street packed with cafes, restaurants, and Cambodian cuisine for food lovers.
🎨
Cambodian Living Arts
Watch live performances of traditional Cambodian music, dance, and shadow puppetry.
🌉
Kizuna Bridge (Japanese Bridge)
A landmark bridge connecting Phnom Penh to Koh Dach island, great for scenic views.
