Kampong Cham

Khmer name: កំពង់ចាម
Capital: Kampong Cham
Population: 1,688,428
Area: 9,799 km²
About

Kampong Cham is one of Cambodia's most populated provinces, situated along the Mekong River in the eastern part of the country. It is known for its rubber plantations, ancient temples, beautiful river islands, and well-preserved French colonial buildings. The province is a major agricultural and economic hub.

What it is known for
  • 🌿 Rubber Plantations
  • 🏯 Wat Nokor Temple
  • 🏝️ Koh Paen Island
  • 🏛️ French Colonial Architecture
📍 Top Places to Visit
🏯
Wat Nokor
An 11th-century Angkor-era sandstone temple uniquely fused with a modern Buddhist pagoda built inside it.
🏝️
Koh Paen Island
A peaceful river island connected by a bamboo bridge, known for cycling, fruit farms, and local village life.
🌉
Bamboo Bridge
A seasonal hand-built bamboo bridge across the Mekong — one of the most unique bridges in Cambodia.
🌿
Rubber Plantations
Tour the vast rubber tree estates and learn about Cambodia's important rubber industry up close.
🏛️
French Colonial Riverside
Stroll along the Mekong riverfront lined with charming French-era buildings and local cafes.
⛩️
Phnom Pros & Phnom Srei
Two sacred hills — "Man Hill" and "Woman Hill" — with temples and a popular local legend.
🛶
Mekong River Cruises
Take a boat along the mighty Mekong and watch daily life unfold along the riverbanks.
🏝️
Koh Dambang Island
A quiet river island perfect for relaxing, exploring by bicycle, and escaping city life.
🌅
Kampong Cham Riverfront
A scenic promenade ideal for evening walks, street food, and watching beautiful Mekong sunsets.
🕌
Local Markets
Explore bustling local markets filled with fresh produce, Khmer snacks, and everyday provincial life.
