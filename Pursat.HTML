About

Pursat is a province in west-central Cambodia, stretching from the Tonle Sap Lake to the Cardamom Mountains. It is known for its marble carving craftsmanship, rich biodiversity, and dense rainforests. The province offers a mix of nature, wildlife conservation, and traditional Khmer crafts.

What it is known for
  • ⛰ Cardamom Mountains
  • 🪨 Marble Carving
  • 🌊 Tonle Sap Lake
  • 🦎 Wildlife Conservation
📍 Top Places to Visit
⛰
Cardamom Mountains
One of Southeast Asia's largest remaining rainforests, teeming with rare wildlife and trekking routes.
🌊
Tonle Sap Floodplains
Seasonal wetlands along Tonle Sap Lake supporting fishing communities and diverse ecosystems.
🪨
Marble Village (Kbal Taol)
A village renowned for intricate marble and stone carvings, a proud Pursat tradition.
💧
Kanchorp Waterfall
A scenic waterfall nestled in the foothills of the Cardamom Mountains, popular for day trips.
🐆
Central Cardamom Protected Forest
A massive conservation area home to leopards, elephants, and hundreds of plant species.
🛕
Wat Bakan
An ancient hilltop temple with sweeping views over the plains — a sacred pilgrimage site.
🚣
Floating Villages
Communities that live on the water along Tonle Sap, offering a unique cultural experience.
🌾
Rural Countryside
Peaceful farmlands and small villages where traditional Khmer rural life thrives.
